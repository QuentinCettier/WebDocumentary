Smart City - Collaborative City
Issues of data-oriented communities
To succeed in this vision in which the collaborative becomes a shared and virtuous way of life, it seems necessary to me not to neglect the digital exclusion related to financial issues, that strikes a large number of people.
It seems obvious that the city of tomorrow will only be truly Smart if the collaborative does not remain confined to financial exchanges. Part of this collaborative must remain carried by the heart, by good will, and by the desire to help others.
Participatory democracy is no longer an empty word in Western cities, especially in North America where more and more citizens are reappropriating the managementand even the planning of public space.
Whether these citizen actions are spontaneous and empirical or carried out in consultation with public authorities and in connection with innovative methodological tools,they reveal a new trend, with serious consequences for urban development in future years.
Communities and professionals will henceforth have to rely on the creativity of users to think of a layout adapted to their practices.

Contributing with technology
In smart city, initiatives abound: our living spaces, our cities, our territories are becoming more intelligent. We, the inhabitants of these cities, will be able to access many data in real time (air quality, energy, transport...) and the necessary tools to understand them and adjust our uses. But without pooling resources, without organising solidarity and without steering a virtuous and participatory approach, the equation is incomplete.

A totally or partially self-sufficient city is a city capable of satisfying, to ensure its longevity, the basic needs of its users locally without imposing its development costs on other territories. These needs may be of a food, energy or even political nature.
The self-sufficient city poses the problem of the articulation between the global environmental stakes and the logics proper to its development.
Indeed, if a city does not wish to have a negative impact on its global environment, can it sustainably implement a strategy of change that will balance its consumption and production, reduce its dependencies, and even effectively counter the threats weighing on it?

Issues of self-sufficient cities
The main problem with self-sufficient cities is that not all regions have the same agricultural capacity and demographic constraints. Thus disparities will inevitably emerge, creating a hierarchy of cities that could thus lead to major political problems.
These self-sufficient cities take the risk of serving as showcases of a modern and rich world in the face of some other cities that are not as developed, and thus reinforce socio-spatial inequalities.
An ambitious initiative
Much more ambitious than a simple support to short circuits and to local agriculture, a policy aiming at food self-sufficiency supposes an important commitment of all the actors of the territory, from producers to consumers.
The driving force of an intelligent food relocation dynamic.
Food self-sufficiency seeks to save land while optimizing production according to soil type, climate and minimizing environmental impacts throughout the food supply chain, as this is where the potential for agricultural relocation and short circuit development lies.
A pretext for the creativity of territories
It must pave the way for an awareness of the impact of our consumption on the environment and encourage a more sustainable agricultural production method.
However, the services that agriculture can provide to the city in terms of living environment are constrained by a necessary geographical proximity which is nowadays complicated to set up.

Life in Autarky
Some countries or cities decide to try to live in complete autonomy, that is to say that this area is able to provide for all its needs without calling on any external resources. It is a closed economy. However, today this idea remains utopian: no country or state has managed to establish a perfectly autarkic system, even the most closed countries like North Korea.
